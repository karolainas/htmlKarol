Great Albums

In the truest sense, this is a must-listen record; this is not because it constructed of brilliantly catchy hooks, acrobatic vocal performances, or flashy technical proficiency, but because the message it carries is one of vital importance.
Before discussing the album itself, I would briefly like to address the terrible situation in Gaza. It’s been a year now and it’s honestly mind-boggling how eagerly the governments of the western world turn a blind eye to the atrocities that are committed right before our very eyes. In an age of liberation, equality and humanitarianism, how can we allow such miscarriage of human rights to flourish when we have the power to stop it? You’re deluding yourself if you still consider this to be a political issue: the Israeli government is committing unspeakable crimes upon innocent children and neither the terrible acts made by Hamas nor anything else can excuse such reckless cruelty.
